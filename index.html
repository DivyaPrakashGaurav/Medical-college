Frequently Asked Questions

What are the admission requirements for medical colleges?

Admission requirements vary by institution but typically include a bachelor's degree, completion of specific prerequisite courses, a competitive GPA, MCAT scores, letters of recommendation, and relevant extracurricular activities or work experience.

How long does it take to complete medical school?

Medical school typically takes 4 years to complete. This is followed by a residency program which can range from 3 to 7 years depending on the specialty chosen.

What is the difference between MD and DO programs?

Both MD (Doctor of Medicine) and DO (Doctor of Osteopathic Medicine) are medical degrees that allow physicians to practice medicine. DO programs typically include additional training in osteopathic manipulative treatment and place a stronger emphasis on holistic medicine.

How important is medical school ranking?

While rankings can provide some insight into a school's reputation and resources, they shouldn't be the sole factor in your decision. Consider curriculum, location, cost, culture, and opportunities for specialization when choosing a medical school.

What financial aid options are available for medical students?

Medical students can access various financial aid options including federal loans, institutional scholarships, private loans, and service-based programs like the National Health Service Corps Scholarship which provides funding in exchange for service in underserved areas.
